Understanding Groundwater in the Ti Tree Basin

This project brings together Aboriginal knowledge and hydrogeological science to understand groundwater systems in the Ti Tree palaeovalley and support sustainable water management.

Project Area

The Aboriginal Water Science project focuses on groundwater systems in the Ti Tree Basin in Central Australia. The region relies heavily on groundwater from palaeovalley aquifers that store water within layers of sand and gravel.

Understanding these groundwater systems helps protect water resources for communities, ecosystems and future generations.

The Science

Groundwater in the Ti Tree Basin occurs mainly in sand and gravel layers within an ancient palaeovalley system. These sediments form aquifers that can store and transmit groundwater.

  • Rainfall infiltrates through the soil and recharges groundwater.
  • Water moves slowly through sand and gravel layers.
  • Groundwater supports springs, waterholes and ecosystems.

Field Investigations

The project includes several types of field investigations:

  • Groundwater level monitoring
  • Water quality sampling
  • Electromagnetic geophysics surveys
  • Sediment sampling in Allungra Creek

Preliminary Results

Water quality sampling indicates spatial variability in groundwater chemistry across the basin. Nitrate concentrations vary between locations and may be influenced by land use, hydrogeology and local environmental conditions.
